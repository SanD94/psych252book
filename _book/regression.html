Chapter 7 Regression

7.1 Resources

Much of the material in this chapter was directly copied or slightly adapted from here Broadening Your Statistical Horizons Online Book

Ordinary least squares (OLS) assumptions:

  • Linear: linear relationship between explanatory variable (X) and response (Y)
  • __I__ndependent: observations are independent of one another
  • N ormal: responses are approximately normally distributed for each level of X
  • E qual: variation in the responses is the same for each level of X –> homogeneity of variance

Figure 7.1: Ordinary least squares assumptions

Generalized linear models (GLM) assumptions:

  • not required:
    • linear relationship between explanatory variable (X) and response (Y)
    • responses are approximately normally distributed for each level of X –> responses can be counts or binary
    • homogeneity of variance
  • still required:
    • observations are independent of one another

Multilevel Methods are appropriate for correlated data (e.g. several observations from the same participant)
